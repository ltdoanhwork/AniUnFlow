\section{Branch-Level Quantitative Summary}

The emphasis of this chapter is not a single benchmark headline, but the behavior of AniUnFlow as a framework. The most useful quantitative view for this thesis is therefore a compact branch-level comparison showing how the object-memory idea changed as the framework added dense refinement, propagation memory, and finally a more aggressive large-motion extension.

\begin{table*}[t]
\centering
\caption{\textbf{Representative validation summary across the main AniUnFlow development path.} The values are included as lightweight branch-level evidence rather than as the sole basis of the thesis. Lower EPE is better; higher threshold accuracy is better.}
\label{tab:branch_progression}
\resizebox{\textwidth}{!}{%
\begin{tabular}{lccccccc}
\toprule
\textbf{AniUnFlow branch} & \textbf{EPE $\downarrow$} & \textbf{Acc@1px $\uparrow$} & \textbf{Acc@3px $\uparrow$} & \textbf{Acc@5px $\uparrow$} & \boldmath{$s<10$} & \boldmath{$10\le s < 50$} & \boldmath{$s>50$} \\
\midrule
Object-memory baseline & 10.03 & 0.155 & 0.414 & 0.534 & 3.87 & 19.95 & 90.89 \\
Dense object-memory & 9.26 & 0.225 & 0.461 & 0.576 & 3.10 & 19.11 & 90.56 \\
\rowcolor{gray!10}
Propagation-memory AniUnFlow & 9.57 & 0.161 & 0.430 & 0.550 & 3.42 & 19.40 & 90.85 \\
Large-motion extension & 10.46 & 0.034 & 0.249 & 0.486 & 4.67 & 19.07 & 90.47 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}}
\end{table*}

Table~\ref{tab:branch_progression} should be interpreted carefully. It is not meant to claim that every later branch dominates every earlier branch on every accessible checkpoint. Instead, it helps explain the research direction of AniUnFlow. The dense object-memory branch improves threshold accuracy by letting local matching restore articulated detail that the slot prior alone cannot explain. The propagation-memory branch then shifts the emphasis from raw aggressiveness to \emph{stability}: it injects a more conservative structural signal after motion prediction, helping the framework decide where dense updates should be trusted. The large-motion extension broadens the search strategy further, but it is best read as the newest exploratory direction rather than as the main thesis model.

This reading is consistent with the design logic of the framework. The early object-memory branch proved that segmentation-aware slots and temporal slot memory create a coherent structural prior. Dense refinement improved local geometry, but also created a risk that the dense branch would overwhelm the object prior. Propagation memory was introduced precisely to regulate that tension by telling the refiner where structure propagated from nearby frames remains reliable. In other words, the later AniUnFlow design is not simply ``more modules''; it is a stronger attempt to make structure and dense correspondence cooperate rather than compete.

\section{Qualitative Visual Analysis}

For animation, qualitative evidence is unusually important because the most visible failure modes are perceptual: ghosted contours, broken limbs, boundary drag, identity swap in flat interiors, and unstable motion around repeated line structure. Scalar averages alone often hide these effects. We therefore place substantial weight on panel-based visual analysis.

\begin{figure*}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{img/aniunflow_compare/cami_02_05_A_comparison_sheet.png}
\caption{\textbf{Representative qualitative comparison for AniUnFlow-style motion estimation.} The comparison sheet illustrates how structure-aware flow differs from weaker alternatives on the same clip. The most important reading is not pixel-perfect color similarity, but whether the predicted motion remains coherent around thin contours, articulated limbs, rigid scene elements, and large flat interiors.}
\label{fig:comparison_sheet}
\end{figure*}

Figure~\ref{fig:comparison_sheet} captures the motivation for the framework in a compact visual form. In difficult regions, the best object-memory predictions do not merely reduce overall error; they preserve a more believable motion organization. Rigid background elements stay less smeared, contour boundaries remain sharper, and articulated foreground regions are less likely to leak across neighboring flat-color areas. These are precisely the traits that matter for animation use cases such as interpolation, temporal stylization, or paint propagation.

Figure~\ref{fig:ablation_component_diagnostics} explains why AniUnFlow is built as a staged framework instead of a single dense black box. The structured prior already captures the broad motion arrangement, which is valuable in stylized scenes where local evidence is weak. However, the prior alone remains too smooth for thin structures and articulated motion. The dense stage restores that local geometry, and the boundary-aware residual then spends its capacity on the small set of pixels where even the dense prior is still uncertain. This decomposition is visually easier to understand than through endpoint error alone, and it is one of the main reasons the framework reads more naturally as a paper.

\section{Efficiency and Deployability}

Since the current thesis emphasizes writing and visualization more than exhaustive runtime benchmarking, we summarize deployability through \emph{design-level efficiency proxies}. These are still useful because they reveal where AniUnFlow spends computation and which additions are likely to increase memory or latency.

\begin{tabular}{lcccc}
\toprule

\rowcolor{gray!10}
Propagation-memory AniUnFlow & yes & 2 & propagated SAM agreement & Slightly higher engineering complexity, stronger stability bias \\

The deployability lesson is simple. AniUnFlow remains attractive because the main framework does not require abandoning local refinement or rebuilding the entire training stack around a heavy global matcher. Instead, it upgrades a structure-first object-memory pipeline with a propagation-memory cue that is easy to reason about and easy to visualize. The latest large-motion extension is more ambitious, but it is also more expensive and more exploratory.

\section{Latest Large-Motion Extension}

The latest extension to AniUnFlow pushes the framework toward harder long-range motion. Rather than relying purely on a locally refined prior, it introduces three new ideas: non-rigid slot flow, a coarse global search stage, and visibility-aware compositing.

The extension is important for the thesis because it shows that AniUnFlow is not a dead-end architecture. The same structure-first principles that motivated the main framework can naturally expand toward larger search spaces and harder motion regimes. At the same time, keeping this material as a short extension rather than the main story makes the thesis cleaner: the reader first understands the core framework, then sees how it can be extended when large displacement becomes the dominant failure mode.
